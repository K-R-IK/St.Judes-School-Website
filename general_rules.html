Standards that ensure a safe, respectful, and productive learning environment.

Rules

  1. Students are urged to contribute to the high standard of the School by their manners and general behaviour. For smooth running, no student should bring objects that cause disturbance.
  2. Posture, cleanliness, silence, politeness and respect — the school is a place of reverence and discipline; students must reflect this in conduct toward teachers and peers.
  3. Absolute silence must be maintained in teaching hours — in the library, labs, computer room, assembly or while moving between classes.
  4. Respect school and others’ property. Furniture, teaching aids and charts must be returned to their places after use. Any damage must be made good.
  5. English is the medium on the premises. Students must use English to improve proficiency and maintain a high standard of conversation.
  6. No unauthorized items. Books, newspapers, games, cell phones, radios, watches or electrical instruments not related to school work must not be brought without permission. Items with a bad influence will be confiscated; students may be punished.
    Penalty: Possession of a phone, wrist watch or electronic gadget — fine of ₹500 and device confiscated and retained for the academic year. Repeat offence: fine doubled and device destroyed; further offence may lead to expulsion.
  7. School is not responsible for personal losses. Students should avoid bringing valuables. If items are brought for genuine reasons they must be handed to the Principal/Headmistress or a teacher and collected after school.
  8. Discipline measures. The Principal may chastise for habitual idleness, disobedience or misconduct, detain after hours, impose minor punishments, or in serious cases dismiss or ask a student to discontinue.
  9. No collections or fines without prior sanction of the Principal. Presentation of gifts to staff also requires prior sanction.
  10. Class monitor responsibility. In the teacher’s absence the class monitor/monitress maintains order with loyalty and impartiality.
  11. Assigned responsibilities must be executed dutifully and impartially as directed by the Principal or deputed teacher.
  12. Individual approach to the Principal. Students may approach the Principal individually for reasonable or sympathetic consideration — not in groups.
  13. No private tuition by staff without written permission from the Principal. Such tuitions must stop one week before examinations.
  14. Assembly procedures. When the first bell rings, students must get into class queues for Assembly to pray and listen silently to announcements.
  15. Attendance at Assembly. Every student should attend Assembly to begin the day with prayer and receive important announcements.
  16. Be courteous and cooperative. Treat all students with respect and behave in a dignified manner.
  17. Come prepared and in proper attire. Arrive on time, neatly dressed, with lessons prepared and homework done. Bring text and material required for the day’s routine.
  18. Keep classrooms and the compound clean. Use bins provided for waste — maintain spotlessly clean premises.
  19. Staff-room access. Students are not allowed to visit the staff-room without prior permission of the Principal or a teacher.
  20. Serious misconduct. Persistent insubordination, willful mischief, examination malpractice, or behaviour judged by the Principal to be an unwholesome influence may result in temporary removal or permanent expulsion.
  21. Assault or demoralizing behaviour. Assaulting a teacher, serious attacks on students, or persistent rude behaviour may lead to permanent removal.
  22. Punctuality & entry. Repeated lateness, forgetting books or coming without proper uniform may be punished and fined. Students arriving five minutes after Assembly will not be allowed entry.
