School Policies

ST. JUDE'S SCHOOL is committed to creating a safe, inclusive, and productive learning environment. Our policies guide students, staff, and parents to maintain standards of behavior, safety, and academic integrity.

1. Academic Policies

  • Attendance: Students are required to maintain at least 90% attendance. Parents must inform the school in case of absences.
  • Examination Rules: All exams must be attempted honestly; plagiarism or malpractice is strictly prohibited.
  • Homework and Assignments: Assignments must be submitted on time. Late submissions may incur grade penalties.
  • Promotion Criteria: Students must meet academic benchmarks to progress to the next grade level.

2. Code of Conduct

  • Respect: Students must respect peers, teachers, staff, and school property.
  • Discipline: Bullying, harassment, or violence will not be tolerated. Disciplinary measures may include warnings, detention, or suspension.
  • Dress Code: Students must wear the prescribed school uniform. Proper grooming and hygiene are mandatory.

3. Safety and Security Policies

  • Visitor Policy: All visitors must sign in at the reception and follow safety protocols.
  • Emergency Protocols: Fire drills and safety evacuations are conducted regularly. Students must follow instructions promptly.
  • Health & Hygiene: Regular health check-ups and vaccination records are maintained. Students must adhere to hygiene guidelines.

4. Technology and Internet Use

  • Acceptable Use: Students may use school devices and internet access for academic purposes only.
  • Cyber Safety: Sharing inappropriate content, hacking, or online harassment is strictly prohibited.
  • Monitoring: The school reserves the right to monitor all device usage on campus.

5. Parent Involvement Policies

  • Parent-Teacher Meetings: Regular meetings are conducted to discuss student progress.
  • Volunteering: Parents are encouraged to participate in school activities and community events.
  • Feedback: Suggestions regarding school policies can be submitted through the feedback portal.

6. Grievance Redressal

Students or parents with concerns about school policies or practices may contact the principal or designated staff member. All complaints will be addressed promptly and confidentially.
