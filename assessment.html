Assessment & Evaluation

Our assessment framework is designed to drive consistent learning outcomes by combining structured evaluation, continuous feedback, and strong academic discipline.

1. Assessment Philosophy

Our evaluation model reinforces conceptual clarity, consistent practice, and strong academic habits. We focus on tracking progress over time instead of one-time testing.

2. Types of Assessments

Formative Assessments

  • Regular class tests
  • Oral assessments & recitations
  • Homework reviews
  • Activity-based evaluations

Summative Assessments

  • Mid-term examinations
  • Annual examinations
  • Project submissions
  • Practical exams (Science & Computer)

3. Grading System

Grades reflect overall academic achievement, discipline, and participation. Each grade indicates the learner’s mastery level in both theoretical and practical components.

  • A1 – Outstanding
  • A2 – Excellent
  • B1 – Very Good
  • B2 – Good
  • C – Satisfactory
  • D – Needs Improvement

4. Promotion Policy

Promotions are based on combined performance in assignments, examinations, attendance, discipline, and overall participation. Continuous support is given to students requiring improvement.

5. Parent–Teacher Interaction

Transparent communication is the backbone of improvement. Parent–Teacher Meetings (PTMs) are held regularly to review progress and discuss academic strategies.
